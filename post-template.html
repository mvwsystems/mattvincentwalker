A Major Section Heading

Use bold for terms or phrases that carry real weight — not for decoration. Use italics for titles, foreign phrases, or a word you want to land with precision. Neither should appear more than once or twice per page.

A Sub-Section If You Need One

Sub-sections should be used sparingly. If the essay needs more than three, reconsider the structure. Most pieces that feel like they need five sections actually need tighter paragraphs.

  1. First item — keep it short, keep it specific
  2. Second item — one idea per line, no qualifications
  3. Third item — if the list grows past five, cut until it doesn't
  • A concept, tool, or reference worth naming
  • Another one — no connective tissue needed
  • Lists should feel like a table of contents, not a paragraph in disguise

The closing paragraphs of a piece do one of two things: they complete the thought or they open a door. Never do both. Decide before you write the last line whether you want the reader to feel resolved or restless — and write toward only that.
