The Internal Audit

You've fixed
the problem before.
It came back.
That's not a business problem.

Five questions that have stopped CEOs mid-sentence. Read them slowly. Alone.

Most leaders can tell you exactly what's wrong with their business. Their team. Their systems. Their market. Their time. What they almost never do is ask the question underneath the question — the one that's been running the whole operation from a room they haven't visited in years. These five questions open that door.

01

What problem in your business or life keeps coming back no matter what you do about it?

02

What does it mean about you that it's still there?

03

If it disappeared tomorrow — what are you afraid would still be true?

Sit with this one.

04

When's the first time in your life you felt exactly that way? What happened?

05

How much of your current decision-making is a response to that moment?

If something shifted reading this — even slightly — that wasn't the questions. That was you, finally letting yourself see it. That's the beginning of everything. The next step is a conversation.
